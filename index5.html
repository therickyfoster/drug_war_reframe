Why reframe 'Us vs. Them' as 'Community Capacity Building'?

Answer

Addiction thrives where meaning collapses—places marked by poverty, isolation, unemployment, trauma and hopelessness. Rural health experts note that low educational attainment, poverty, lack of mental‑health care and isolation drive substance use in rural communities【395601033660152†L86-L94】. The National Academy of Medicine’s report on social determinants of overdose explains that poverty, unemployment, housing instability and structural racism contribute to overdose crises and shape access to services【449638355935092†L26-L66】. It recommends investing in housing, income supports, decriminalisation and integrating harm‑reduction services with wraparound social supports【449638355935092†L110-L130】. Community‑driven harm‑reduction programmes embody this approach: they reflect local values, build trust and have reversed hundreds of overdoses. In Lucas County, a community‑led harm‑reduction initiative reduced overdose deaths by 20 %【202207112024450†L160-L165】. Reframing drug use as a symptom of social stress shifts funding from enforcement to community strength—conservatives hear “community resilience,” progressives hear “social support,” and both agree.
